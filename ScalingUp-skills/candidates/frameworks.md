- id: f01
  title: 4D Framework
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "McKinsey has its 7-S Framework for large companies; we have our 4D Framework for growth firms. The framework includes: Driver, Demands, Disciplines, Decisions."
  summary: |
    4D框架（Driver-Demands-Disciplines-Decisions）是Scaling Up的核心思考维度。
    Driver是领导力驱动；Demands是People与Process的平衡；Disciplines是执行三要素
    （优先级/数据/会议节奏）；Decisions是People/Strategy/Execution/Cash四大决策。
  tags: [core-framework, scaling-up, decision-making]

- id: f02
  title: 4 Decisions
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "To scale the business requires getting four key decision sets — People, Strategy, Execution, and Cash — absolutely right, and there are right and wrong answers."
  summary: |
    企业Scaling必须搞定的四个决策领域：People（人）、Strategy（战略）、
    Execution（执行）、Cash（现金）。四个决策互相咬合，缺一不可。
  tags: [core-framework, strategic-thinking, organizational-design]

- id: f03
  title: FACe（功能 Accountability Chart）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第3章 The Leaders
  source_quote: |
    "The FACe tool provides a list of seats (functions) that all organizations must fill. You want to delegate these functions to people who fit your culture and pass two tests: 1. They don't need to be managed. 2. They regularly wow the team with their insights and output."
  summary: |
    FACe是一个功能问责表，列出企业必须填补的职能座位，并为每个职能指定
    一个KPIs和结果指标。确保"对的屁股在对的位置上"。
  tags: [people, organization-design, accountability]

- id: f04
  title: PACe（流程 Accountability Chart）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第3章 The Leaders
  source_quote: |
    "The Process Accountability Chart (PACe) lists who is accountable for each of the four to nine processes that drive the business and how each process will be measured."
  summary: |
    PACe是一个横向流程问责表，列出4-9个核心业务流程，为每个流程指定
    负责人和KPIs。流程横跨职能，是Scaling的关键。
  tags: [process, organization, accountability]

- id: f05
  title: 7 Strata of Strategy
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第7章
  source_quote: |
    "The 7 Strata of Strategy includes: 1. Words You Own (Mindshare), 2. Sandbox and Brand Promises, 3. Brand Promise Guarantee, 4. One-PHRASE Strategy, 5. Differentiating Activities, 6. X-Factor, 7. Profit per X and BHAG."
  summary: |
    七层战略框架：层层递进构建不可复制的战略壁垒。
    从品牌心智占据→目标客户→承诺保证→短句战略→差异化活动→10-100倍
    优势→经济引擎+长期目标。
  tags: [strategy, competitive-advantage, differentiation]

- id: f06
  title: BHAG（Big Hairy Audacious Goal）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Guided by a set of Core Values and a purpose, it chooses a Big Hairy Audacious Goal (BHAG) to achieve in the next 10 to 25 years."
  summary: |
    BHAG是10-25年的宏伟目标，像登顶珠峰一样为企业指引方向。
    它比普通Vision更大胆，是25年不移的地标，为所有决策提供长期上下文。
  tags: [vision, goal-setting, long-term-thinking]

- id: f07
  title: OPSP（One-Page Strategic Plan）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第8章
  source_quote: |
    "If you want everyone on the same page, then you need this page first. The OPSP is designed to drive alignment, accountability, and focus. The body consists of seven columns: Who, What, When, Where, How, Why, Shouldn't."
  summary: |
    单页战略计划：用Who/What/When/Where/How/Why/Shouldn't七列
    驱动战略+执行对齐。前三列是战略思考，后四列是执行计划。
  tags: [strategy, execution, alignment, planning]

- id: f08
  title: SWT分析（Strengths, Weaknesses, Trends）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第8章
  source_quote: |
    "We've augmented SWOT with a tool called SWT — focusing on exploring broader external trends beyond their own industry or geography. It's a powerful tool to spot opportunities before the competition."
  summary: |
    SWT用趋势（Trends）替代机会（Opportunities）和威胁（Threats），
    强迫领导者看向外部行业/地理趋势，避免"内部/行业近视症"。
  tags: [strategic-analysis, trend-thinking, competitive-intelligence]

- id: f09
  title: 领导力三要素（预测/授权/重复）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第2章 Barriers
  source_quote: |
    "As goes the leadership team, so goes the rest of the company. Whatever challenges exist within the organization can be traced to the cohesion of the executive team and its capabilities in prediction, delegation, and repetition."
  summary: |
    领导力的三个核心能力：预测（与市场/客户/员工的频繁互动）、
    授权（放权并建立反馈机制）、重复（持续传递核心信息）。
    三者缺失任何一者都会导致Scaling障碍。
  tags: [leadership, management, growth-barriers]

- id: f10
  title: Growth Paradox框架
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第2章 Barriers
  source_quote: |
    "The belief that as you scale the company — and increase your dream team, prospects, and resources — things should get easier, but they don't. Things actually get harder and more complicated."
  summary: |
    成长悖论：越大应该越轻松，但现实中往往越累。
    原因是复杂度指数增长。理解这个悖论是突破Scaling障碍的第一步。
  tags: [growth, complexity, paradox]

- id: f11
  title: Cash Conversion Cycle（现金转换周期）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第12章 The Cash
  source_quote: |
    "It's a technical term for how long it takes, after you spend a dollar/euro/yen on rent, utilities, payroll, inventory, marketing, etc., for it to make its way through your business model and back into your pocket."
  summary: |
    现金转换周期衡量资金从花出去到回到口袋的时间。
    周期越短，企业现金流越健康。增长越快的公司越容易缺现金。
  tags: [cash-management, financial-metrics, liquidity]

- id: f12
  title: The Power of One（七大财务杠杆）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第14章
  source_quote: |
    "The 7 main financial levers available to managers to improve cash and returns: 1. Price, 2. Volume, 3. Cost of goods sold, 4. Operating expenses, 5. Accounts receivable, 6. Inventory/WIP, 7. Accounts payable."
  summary: |
    七大财务杠杆：每个杠杆+1%或-1天对现金的影响都可以量化。
    工具计算每个杠杆变动对现金的具体改善效果。
  tags: [financial-analysis, cash-flow, leverage]

- id: f13
  title: "Routine Sets You Free"
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Routine sets you free. You may set a goal to lose weight, but unless you change some daily and weekly routines, it will never be accomplished. Goals without routines are wishes; routines without goals are aimless."
  summary: |
    习惯/例程（Routines）体系是Scaling的核心。不是更聪明，而是更重复。
    建立正确的习惯体系才能真正自由，否则只是空有目标却没有行动路径。
  tags: [habits, execution, productivity]

- id: f14
  title: 复杂度方程（Complexity Multiplier）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第2章 Barriers
  source_quote: |
    "Add a third person, and the degree of complexity triples from two to six. Add a fourth, and it quadruples to 24. Expanding from three to four people grows the team only 33%, yet complexity may increase 400%."
  summary: |
    复杂度乘法方程：每增加一个人，复杂度不是线性增长而是指数增长。
    这是Scaling失败的根本原因，也是可以系统性解决的核心问题。
  tags: [complexity, organizational-design, scaling]

- id: f15
  title: Topgrading招聘体系
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team
  source_quote: |
    "Topgrading is a methodology for hiring A Players, co-created by Brad and Geoff Smart. It has a proven track record of helping leaders hire the right person more than 90% of the time vs. the 25% to 60% success rates with feel-good conversations."
  summary: |
    Topgrading是一套招聘A类人才的系统化方法论，包含Job Scorecard、
    序列式行为面试、背景调查（TORC）等工具，大幅提升招聘成功率。
  tags: [hiring, talent, recruitment]

- id: f16
  title: A Player vs B/C Player
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team
  source_quote: |
    "An A Player is someone in the top 10% of the available talent pool who is willing to accept your specific offer. You need to attract the largest and most capable talent pool excited about the job and willing to accept your compensation package."
  summary: |
    A类人才定义：可用人才池中排名前10%且愿意接受你特定offer的人。
    一个A类人才等于三个B/C类人才的生产力。应优先招聘A类人才。
  tags: [hiring, talent, management]

- id: f17
  title: X-Factor优势
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第7章 7 Strata
  source_quote: |
    "What is your X-Factor — a 10 times to 100 times underlying advantage over the competition — that completely wipes out any and all rivals?"
  summary: |
    X-Factor是比竞争对手强10-100倍的底层优势。
    它是战略的核心，必须能真正消灭竞争对手的竞争优势。
  tags: [competitive-advantage, strategy, differentiation]

  title: 10大Rockefeller Habits
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "There are 10 fundamental habits that support the successful execution of your strategy — habits that haven't changed for 100 years since John D. Rockefeller implemented them."

- id: f19
  title: 5大管理者习惯（Manager as Coach）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第5章 Managers
  source_quote: |
    "Great managers must focus on five topics: 1. Help people play to their strengths. 2. Don't demotivate; dehassle. 3. Set clear expectations and provide line of sight. 4. Give recognition and show appreciation. 5. Hire fewer people, but pay them more."

- id: f20
  title: 战略思维团队（The Council/Headlight Team）
  type: framework
  source_chapter: 第7章
  source_quote: |
    "It's helpful to think about strategic planning in terms of two separate teams: strategic thinking and execution planning. Jim Collins refers to the strategic thinking team as 'the council.'"
  summary: |
    战略思考与执行规划分离：3-5人组成"议会"每周开会做战略思考，
    而执行规划由更大团队负责。CEO负责最终决策，但需要多双眼睛帮助导航。
  tags: [strategy, leadership, governance]
