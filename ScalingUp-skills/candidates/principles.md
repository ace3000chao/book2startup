# Scaling Up — 原则候选池 (阶段1产出)

> 原则提取器产出 | 不做筛选，完整保留候选

---

- id: p01
  title: Routine Sets You Free（习惯使人自由）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Routine sets you free. Goals without routines are wishes; routines without goals are aimless."
  summary: |
    习惯（Routines）是Scaling的核心执行机制。不要空谈目标，
    要建立能实现目标的日常和周常习惯体系。
  tags: [habit, execution, productivity]

- id: p02
  title: Right People Doing the Right Things Right（对的人做对的事）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Do you have the 'right people doing the right things right' inside the organization?"
  summary: |
    Scaling的核心是确保"对的人"在做"对的事"且"做的方式正确"。
    三个要素缺一不可。
  tags: [people, execution, quality]

- id: p03
  title: Growth Sucks Cash（增长吃现金）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview / 第12章
  source_quote: |
    "Growth sucks cash. This is the first law of entrepreneurial gravity."
  summary: |
    增长是创业引力的第一定律。增长越快，现金消耗越多。
    创业者必须像关注利润一样关注现金流。
  tags: [cash, growth, financial-management]

- id: p04
  title: "Dumbest in the Room"（团队里最笨的人）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Senior leaders know they have succeeded in building an organization that can scale when they are the dumbest people in the room."
  summary: |
    当你成为团队里最笨的人时，说明你已经建立了能Scale的组织。
    如果你总是有答案或假装有答案，组织就会陷入沉默。
  tags: [leadership, delegation, culture]

- id: p05
  title: 3倍行业平均利润率
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Most important, the company is generating less than three times industry average profitability."
  summary: |
    Scaling成功的企业应实现3倍于行业平均的利润率。
    低于这个标准说明执行有严重问题。
  tags: [profitability, execution, benchmark]

- id: p06
  title: 领导力三要素：预测、授权、重复
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第2章 Barriers
  source_quote: |
    "The challenges exist within the organization can be traced to the cohesion of the executive team and its capabilities in prediction, delegation, and repetition."
  summary: |
    领导力的三个核心能力缺失任何一者都会导致Scaling障碍：
    预测不足、与市场脱节；授权不足、瓶颈在顶层；
    重复不足、信息不一致。
  tags: [leadership, management, growth]

- id: p07
  title: 习惯必须季度轮换（每次只推1-2个）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "You'll drive everyone crazy if you implement all these habits at one time. The key is focusing on one or two each quarter, giving everyone roughly 24 to 36 months to install these simple routines."
  summary: |
    不要同时推行所有Rockefeller Habits。每次只聚焦1-2个习惯，
    给团队24-36个月消化，然后再进入下一轮。欲速则不达。
  tags: [execution, habits, change-management]

- id: p08
  title: 复杂度是Scaling的敌人
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第2章 Barriers
  source_quote: |
    "The complexity just keeps growing exponentially. It's why many business owners often long for the day when the company was just them and an assistant."
  summary: |
    每增加一个员工，复杂度指数增长而非线性增长。
    保持简单是克服Scaling障碍的第一原则。
  tags: [complexity, organizational-design]

- id: p09
  title:hire for culture fit（为文化契合度招聘）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team
  source_quote: |
    "It's more important to hire for culture fit than for specific skills. Cultures are like immune systems and will spit out very capable people who don't align with its norms."
  summary: |
    招聘时文化契合度（Core Values匹配）比技能更重要。
    技能可以培训，但文化契合度是天生的。
  tags: [hiring, culture, values]

- id: p10
  title: fewer people, higher pay（少人高薪）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team / 第5章
  source_quote: |
    "Hire fewer people, but pay them more. One great person equals three good people in terms of business productivity."
  summary: |
    用更高薪资吸引更少但更优秀的人才。
    一个A类人才等于三个B/C类人才，且更低的总工资占比反而提升利润。
  tags: [hiring, compensation, productivity]

- id: p11
  title: 招人是营销职能
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team
  source_quote: |
    "The marketing team must be as actively involved in recruiting a steady stream of potential employees as it is in attracting potential customers."
  summary: |
    招聘应该像营销一样主动出击，而不是坐等人才上门。
    需要20:1的申请者-职位比例才能确保招到A类人才。
  tags: [hiring, marketing, recruitment]

- id: p12
  title: Will > Values > Results > Skills（招聘四维优先级）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team
  source_quote: |
    "Will — a desire to excel, act with courage, persevere, learn, and innovate. Values — do they align with your core values. Results — can they deliver on KPIs. Skills — least important, most skill-sets need updating every 5 years."
  summary: |
    招聘四维优先级：意愿第一、价值观第二、结果第三、技能第四。
    技能最容易过时，不应该成为招聘的主要标准。
  tags: [hiring, talent, priority]

- id: p13
  title: Topgrading序列式面试
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第4章 The Team
  source_quote: |
    "The Topgrading methodology requires a three-to four-hour interview going through the candidate's entire work history, asking about each previous boss (with spelling!) to verify references."
  summary: |
    Topgrading要求3-4小时的深度序列面试，回溯候选人完整工作经历。
    短面试无法暴露问题，只会让B/C类人才蒙混过关。
  tags: [hiring, interview, topgrading]

- id: p14
  title: Don't Copy Someone Else's Compensation System（不要复制别人的薪酬体系）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第5章 Managers
  source_quote: |
    "Don't copy somebody else's compensation system. Look to your Core Values, your business model, and your Brand Promise, and let them instruct you in the design of your compensation plan."
  summary: |
    薪酬体系必须基于自身文化、价值观和品牌承诺设计，
    而非简单复制行业标准或最佳实践。
  tags: [compensation, culture, strategy]

- id: p15
  title: Fairness ≠ Sameness（公平不等于相同）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第5章 Managers
  source_quote: |
    "'Fairness' does not mean 'sameness.' You need to be creative and flexible in order to keep your top talent happy, from a compensation-package perspective."
  summary: |
    对待顶尖人才要有个性化的激励方案，而非"公平地"给所有人相同待遇。
    公平在于给每个人他需要的东西，而非相同的的东西。
  tags: [compensation, talent, management]

- id: p16
  title: 3:1正向互动比率
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第5章 Managers
  source_quote: |
    "For people to be happy and productive at work, they need to experience positive interactions (appreciation, praise) vs. negative (reprimands, criticism) with their manager in a ratio of at least 3:1."
  summary: |
    要保持团队快乐和高产，管理者的正向互动（认可/表扬）
    与负向互动（批评/指责）比例至少要达到3:1。
  tags: [management, motivation, relationships]

- id: p17
  title: Don't Demotivate; Dehassle（不要打击，要去障碍）
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第5章 Managers
  source_quote: |
    "The best managers are less concerned about motivating their people and more concerned about NOT demotivating them. They consider it their job to prevent the hassles that block their team's performance."
  summary: |
    优秀管理者不是不断激励，而是消除那些让团队无法发挥的障碍。
    #1去障碍因素是：不要让A类人才和B/C类人才一起工作。
  tags: [management, motivation, productivity]

- id: p18
  title: 战略不能过度复杂
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第7章 7 Strata
  source_quote: |
    "We know that it takes a 'village of gurus' to help a company and that no one person has all the answers."
  summary: |
    没有人能独自搞定战略。必须组建"智囊团"，借助外部顾问、
    导师和团队的集体智慧来制定和优化战略。
  tags: [strategy, leadership, decision-making]

- id: p19
  title: 战略必须通过两个测试
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "You don't have a real strategy if it doesn't pass two tests: First, what you're planning to do really matters to enough customers; and second, it differentiates you from your competition."
  summary: |
    Gary Hamel的定义：真正的战略必须满足两个测试——
    对足够多的客户真的重要，且能差异化于竞争对手。
  tags: [strategy, competitive-advantage, differentiation]

- id: p20
  title: BHAG必须是25年不移的地标
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Everything in between this quarter and the next 10 to 25 years is a WAG: a wild-ankle guess! There are no straight lines in nature or business."
  summary: |
    BHAG是10-25年的方向，不是1-3年的目标。
    中间的所有计划都是"野生猜测"（WAG），要接受不确定性，
    但BHAG方向不能动摇。
  tags: [vision, goal-setting, long-term]

- id: p21
  title: 战略必须每周讨论
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第7章
  source_quote: |
    "It's not sufficient to schedule strategic thinking time once every quarter or year. It's all about iterations: making a few decisions, testing them, and coming back to the table the following week."
  summary: |
    战略思考不能每季度或每年一次。必须每周迭代：
    做决策→测试→回来讨论→再决策。碎片化的战略会议远好过年度规划。
  tags: [strategy, meeting, iteration]

- id: p22
  title: 战略是差异化， 执行不是
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第7章
  source_quote: |
    "You can imitate most elements of strategy. But your 7 Strata — X-Factor, Brand Promise Guarantee, One-PHRASE Strategy — if well designed, are hard to copy."
  summary: |
    执行可以复制，但战略很难复制。如果你的战略很容易被抄走，
    说明你还没有找到真正的X-Factor。
  tags: [strategy, competitive-advantage, differentiation]

- id: p23
  title: 每周识别一个"停止"项
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Each senior leader formally talk to one employee each week and ask, 'What should the company Start/Stop/Keep doing?' Pay particular attention to the 'stops.'"
  summary: |
    每周从员工那里收集Start/Stop/Keep反馈，
    特别关注"Stop"——这些是必须清除的团队障碍。
  tags: [feedback, continuous-improvement, leadership]

- id: p24
  title: 客户投诉是最好的创新信号
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Each senior leader formally ask customers questions that are more about gathering market intel, especially about competitors, than discerning whether they like your particular product."
  summary: |
    主动向客户收集市场情报，特别是竞争对手动态，
    比问他们是否喜欢你的产品更有价值。
  tags: [customer-feedback, market-intelligence, innovation]

- id: p25
  title: 一页纸驱动全员对齐
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第8章 OPSP
  source_quote: |
    "If you want everyone on the same page, then you need this page first."
  summary: |
    如果你想让全员对齐战略和执行，必须先有一页纸的工具
    让所有人能快速理解和参照。
  tags: [alignment, communication, strategy]

- id: p26
  title: 少即是多：优先级要少
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第9章 Priority
  source_quote: |
    "Set a handful of Priorities (the fewer the better)."
  summary: |
    优先事项越少越好。季度主题最好只有1个。
    什么都重要=什么都不重要。
  tags: [priorities, focus, execution]

- id: p27
  title: 数据驱动决策，但不要数据过载
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第10章 Data
  source_quote: |
    "Gather quantitative and qualitative Data daily and review weekly to guide decisions."
  summary: |
    每天收集量化和质化数据，每周复盘指导决策。
    但指标不要过多，每职能1-2个核心KPI足够。
  tags: [data, decision-making, KPIs]

- id: p28
  title: 快速成长组织有更快的脉搏
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第1章 Overview
  source_quote: |
    "Those who pulse faster, grow faster."
  summary: |
    组织的心跳（会议节奏）越快，成长越快。
    日站会→周例会→月复盘→季规划→年审视，缺一不可。
  tags: [meeting-rhythm, growth, cadence]

- id: p29
  title: 现金流比利润更重要
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第12章 The Cash
  source_quote: |
    "Paying attention to how every decision affects cash flow as you would to revenue and profitability."
  summary: |
    现金流是企业的命脉。决策时必须同时评估对现金流的影响，
    而不仅仅关注收入和利润数字。
  tags: [cash, financial-management, decision-making]

- id: p30
  title: 一流人才需要一流教练
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第5章
  source_quote: |
    "No one has ever achieved peak performance without a coach."
  summary: |
    所有顶尖表现者都有教练。领导力成长、
    团队管理和个人绩效提升都需要外部教练的支持。
  tags: [coaching, leadership, performance]

- id: p31
  title: 入职即文化灌输开始
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第5章 Managers
  source_quote: |
    "A proper onboarding process makes new hires feel welcome and integrates them more quickly into the culture. Design the process around the teaching of your Values and Purpose."
  summary: |
    入职第一天就开始文化灌输，而非入职几周后才开始。
    用价值观和使命来组织整个入职流程。
  tags: [onboarding, culture, values]

- id: p32
  title: 所有HR系统必须对齐Core Values
  type: principle
  source_chapter: 第6章 The Core
  source_quote: |
    "A leader must go beyond merely posting a company's Values and Purpose on the wall. The key is for you to align ALL of your HR systems and processes around one list of your Values."
  summary: |
    Core Values不能只是墙上标语。必须将所有HR系统
    （招聘/绩效/奖励/手册/入职）都对齐到同一套价值观。
  tags: [core-values, HR, culture]
